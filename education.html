Introduction

Renewable energy comes from natural resources like sunlight, wind, rain, tides, and geothermal heat, which are naturally replenished. They are important for reducing carbon emissions and protecting the environment.

Solar Energy

Solar energy harnesses sunlight and converts it into usable electricity. Solar panels are the most common technology used to capture solar energy.

Wind Energy

Wind energy involves using wind turbines to capture the kinetic energy of wind and convert it into electricity. It's a clean and renewable energy source.

Hydropower

Hydropower generates electricity by using the gravitational force of falling or flowing water. Dams are commonly used for this purpose.

Geothermal Energy

Geothermal energy taps into the Earth's internal heat to generate electricity. It's a reliable and sustainable energy source.

Biomass Energy

Biomass energy is derived from organic materials like wood, agricultural residues, and waste. It can be used to produce electricity, heat, and biofuels.

Tidal Energy

Tidal energy harnesses the power of ocean tides to generate electricity. It works by using turbines placed in tidal streams.
